Role:

  • UX/UI designer

Problem:

Musly is a Latvia based Museum guide + exhibition app, that provides the museums with an opportunity to cut on Audio guide technology costs and make the exhibitions more interactive. Since the project, on the moment of my involvement, was fairly new, it's product website consisted of a single page with basic information. This created an unprofessional feel of the company and didn't talk enough about the product.

Solution:

Solution consists of a redesigned and improved website, that talks more about the product, as well as showcases it's design and gives a sight at it's development process.

Old website design:

Problems with the old design:

Some of the problems I found in the old Musly website were:

  • Inconsistency with buttons - buttons don't fit the design, as well as are not consistent throughout the page
  • The information about the product was in a separate presentation that a user would have to go through (roughly 23 slides) in order to understand what is the producs is actually about.
  • Inconsistent formatting of elements.
  • No contact form.

Snippets of the new design:

Improvements:

  • An About page was added, that transformed the initial Powerpoint presentation into a comprehensive page that explains the thought behind the product and how everythings works.
  • Formatting was made consistent throughout the website and the pages were ensured to be mobile responsive.
  • A pricing policy page was added, making it easy for museum representatives to see the pricing for the app implementation.
  • A contact form was added.
  • Overall, the website got a more professional loo and developed a cohesive style.
